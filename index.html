Definitions & taxonomy

Benefit

Business impact

  • The headline outcome for the organisation. This is the executive-level impact on cost, risk, quality, or performance.
  • Benefits are not about the product. They're about the business.
  • They answer an organisation's question "Why should we care?"

Value propositions

Experienced gains

  • How the organisation experiences improvement as a result of KV.
  • Value props sit between business impact and implementation. They describe practical gains without explaining how they're delivered.
  • For organisations it answers the question "What actually improves for us?"

Mechanism

How it works

  • The causal engine that makes the value propositions true. This is KV's governance logic, not just a feature set.
  • For KV, the mechanism is unusually central because it represents a shift in training governance.
  • For organisations it answers "How does this actually achieve those gains?"

Features

What exists

  • The functional capabilities in the product that operationalise the mechanism.
  • Features are not value on their own. They are implementation proof.
  • "What does the system do, or allow me to do?"

Why the order matters

We start with Business Impact (Benefit) at the top and trace downward to Features.

This reflects how buyers evaluate products:

  1. What outcome does this create?
  2. What improves in practice?
  3. How does it achieve that?
  4. What functionality makes it possible?

This keeps the focus on organisational impact, not product mechanics.
